Privacy Policy

Tilly · Last updated: 9 June 2026
Plain English summary: Tilly stores all your budget and expense data on your iPhone only. We do not collect, see, or transmit your personal financial information. There is no account, no sign-up, no analytics, and no advertising tracking.

1. Who we are

Tilly is developed and operated by Mark Holmes, a sole trader based in the United Kingdom (the "developer", "we", "us"). If you have any questions about this policy or your data, you can contact us at markh1986@hotmail.com.

2. What data the app processes

2.1 Data stored on your device

When you use Tilly, the following information is created and stored locally on your iPhone using iOS local storage. None of this leaves your device unless you choose to export it yourself:

  • Your monthly budget figure and optional daily spending limit
  • Expense entries (date, amount, category, optional notes)
  • Direct debits and recurring bills you add
  • Savings goals and progress
  • Custom shortcut buttons you create
  • App preferences (currency, theme, pay cycle settings)

2.2 Data we do not collect

We do not collect your name, email address, phone number, contacts, location, photos, device identifiers (IDFA/IDFV) for tracking purposes, advertising data, analytics, crash reports, or usage statistics. There is no user account or login.

3. Third-party services

Tilly integrates with a small number of third parties only where strictly necessary to provide a specific feature. These are the only parties that may receive any data from the app:

3.1 Apple (App Store and In-App Purchases)

When you purchase a subscription or one-time upgrade, the transaction is processed by Apple via the App Store. Apple receives your payment information and Apple ID. We never see your payment details. Apple's privacy policy applies: apple.com/legal/privacy.

3.2 RevenueCat (subscription management)

We use RevenueCat to manage subscription state (i.e. to know whether your Pro subscription is active). RevenueCat receives an anonymous identifier generated by your device, plus your purchase events from Apple. RevenueCat does not receive your name, email, budget data, or expense data. Their privacy policy: revenuecat.com/privacy.

3.3 Cloudflare (request relay – optional AI features only)

The optional AI features (AI Tips and Coach, both Pro) communicate with Anthropic through a small Cloudflare Worker that we operate. The Worker exists solely to hold the Anthropic API key securely on a server rather than embedding it in the iOS app. Cloudflare sees the request in transit, applies rate limiting to control costs, and forwards it to Anthropic. Cloudflare does not retain the request contents or use them for any other purpose. Their privacy policy: cloudflare.com/privacypolicy.

3.4 Anthropic (AI Tips and Coach – optional, Pro only)

Two optional Pro features send data to Anthropic's Claude API to generate personalised responses. Both features are opt-in — if you do not use them, no data is sent to Anthropic at any time.

AI Tips: when you tap the "Get AI Tips" button, a short summary of your spending is sent. The summary includes:

  • Your monthly budget figure (e.g. "£1,500")
  • Total spent this period (e.g. "£432")
  • Spending totals per category (e.g. "Food: £120, Transport: £45")
  • Direct debit names and amounts
  • Savings goal names and percentage progress

Coach (chat): when you send a message in the Coach tab, the following is sent: (a) the same budget summary as above, plus your pay-cycle dates and category names; (b) the question you typed; (c) the recent conversation history with Coach (up to the most recent 10 messages) so Coach can answer in context. We apply a hard limit of 20 Coach messages per device per day to control costs and discourage abuse.

In both features the data sent does not include your name, email, location, individual expense notes, transaction descriptions, or any identifier that could be used to link the data to you as a specific person. Anthropic does not use API requests to train its models. Anthropic's privacy policy: anthropic.com/legal/privacy.

4. International data transfers

RevenueCat, Cloudflare, and Anthropic are based in the United States. When you make a purchase or use the optional AI features, the relevant data is transferred to and processed in the United States. All three providers operate under safeguards that the UK Information Commissioner's Office recognises as adequate for international transfers.

5. Data retention and deletion

Because all your budget data is stored only on your iPhone, you control retention entirely. To delete all your data, simply delete the Tilly app from your iPhone. iOS will automatically remove every piece of data associated with the app. You can also clear individual entries from within the app at any time.

6. Your rights under UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018

You have the right to access, correct, or delete personal data we hold about you, and to object to or restrict processing. Because we do not hold any personal data on our servers (all data sits on your device), most of these rights are exercised directly by you within the app or by deleting the app. If you have questions about Anthropic or RevenueCat's handling of the limited data they process, contact them directly using the links above, or contact us and we will help.

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the UK Information Commissioner's Office at ico.org.uk.

7. Children

Tilly is not directed at children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly process data from children under 13. If you believe a child has used the app, please contact us.

8. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we do, we will update the "Last updated" date at the top. Material changes will be announced in the app or via the App Store update notes.

9. Contact

For any questions about this Privacy Policy, your data, or anything else relating to Tilly, please email markh1986@hotmail.com.
